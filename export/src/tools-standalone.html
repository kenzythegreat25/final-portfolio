How I pick tools

A small, sharp kit beats a big one.

Most operators collect tools the way some people collect tabs — endlessly, defensively. I don't. I'd rather know three platforms cold than fifteen halfway. Here's the bar a tool has to clear before it earns daily use.

01

Earns its slot every week

If I haven't opened it in 14 days, it gets cut. The stack stays lean by design, not accident.

02

Plays well with the rest

Tools that don't integrate cleanly create silent work. I prefer fewer apps over a "best-in-class" graveyard.

03

Beats the alternative on craft, not features

Smartlead over Apollo Sequences because of how replies are handled — not because of a feature checklist.

04

I can teach it in a week

If I can't onboard a new client team to the workflow in 5 working days, the tool's too heavy.
